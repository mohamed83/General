Good & Bad Responses to the Epidemic – The #EbolaReport, September 19, 2014 

September 19, 2014ebolareport
Leave a comment



Tragedy brings out different reactions in different groups of people, so today’s roundup looks at the good and bad of community responses to the epidemic.



Looking for non-monetary ways to volunteer? Information crowdsourcing is providing non-local, high-tech support for the Ebola fight. [Reuters, 9/18/14] …and here’s the project itself, the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team, for anyone interested in helping out: http://hot.openstreetmap.org/projects/west_africa_ebola_epidemic
Guinea: 8 people, including doctors and journalists, murdered while trying to raise Ebola awareness in the southern part of the country [BBC, 9/18/14] 
Prime Minister Fofana promises justice for the murder victims; six suspects arrested [Guinée News (French only), 9/18/14] 
Liberia: WHO doctor urges communities to accept survivors: “these people don’t have the virus any more… they cannot infect anybody.” [The News, 9/19/14] 
Youth group outside Monrovia building cheap, homemade washing units to help mitigate the spread of the virus. [The Inquirer (Monrovia} via AllAfrica, 9/18/14] 
Sierra Leone: how citizens of Freetown are handling the economic impact of the epidemic [IRIN, 9/18/14] 

In other news:

WHO director-general addresses UN, calls Ebola “likely the greatest peacetime challenge that the United Nations and its agencies have ever faced.” [WHO, 9/18/14] 
World Bank issues report assessing the economic impact of the outbreak, with projections for “low” and “high” scenarios. [World Bank, 9/17/14] 

– Culled by Brad Damaré.
