Fresh vaccine can stimulate immune system against Ebola virus



 الاثنين 13 ذو القعدة 1435 - 11:09 بتوقيت مكة المكرمة الموافق  8-9-2014


















                                Washington (IINA) – Scientists have confirmed their success in developing of an experimental vaccine to treat people infected with Ebola. The new vaccine has given “a long-term immunity” against the disease during its initial trials on monkeys, raising hopes for developing successful vaccines for humans.
The experiments conducted on animals by the American National Institute of Health revealed that the immunity conferred by this vaccine reaches up to 10 months. Accordingly, the first human safety study of this vaccine will begin this week in the United States and Britain, in hopes that it will stimulate the immune system against Ebola virus.
The World Health Organization (WHO) said: “More than 2,000 people died as a result of Ebola virus infection in West Africa,” adding that there are several experimental vaccines which can reduce the spread of Ebola, including a vaccine developed by the American National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases.
The National Institute of Health published some of the key animal research behind those injections. One reason the vaccine was deemed promising was that a single dose protected all 4 vaccinated monkeys when they were exposed to high levels of Ebola virus just five weeks later, researchers reported in the journal Nature Medicine.
Dr. Anthony Fauci, director of the U.S. National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases, told BBC: “This vaccine confer full immunity against Ebola virus for 5 weeks or a little less.”
Dr. Fauci said: “The bigger challenge is that the protection wanes over time unless the animals got a booster shot made a different way,” noting that “The success of this vaccine on monkeys is the best available evidence to us, that such a vaccine will be effective on humans.”
The experimental vaccine was given to the first Ebola-infected lady last week, and there will be separate trials of the vaccine on humans. WHO said that healthcare workers will have priority of this vaccine in the future.
